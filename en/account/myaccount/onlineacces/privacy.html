1. Information we collect

We collect information you provide directly and information generated through your use of the service.

  • Account details: name, email, phone number, address, and other information you submit during registration.
  • Verification information: email verification codes and status; in some cases, identity or supporting documents if required by your account workflow.
  • Transaction and service data: records of activities you initiate (e.g., requests, deposits, card actions).
  • Device and usage data: IP address, browser type, timestamps, pages viewed, and error logs.

2. How we use information

We use your information to operate, maintain, improve, and secure the service, including:

  • Creating and managing your account.
  • Providing customer support and responding to requests.
  • Sending service communications (e.g., verification emails, security alerts).
  • Preventing fraud, abuse, and unauthorized access.
  • Monitoring performance and debugging issues.
  • Complying with legal obligations where applicable.

3. How we share information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share data in these situations:

  • Service providers: vendors that help us run the service (e.g., email delivery, hosting, media storage).
  • Legal requests: if required to comply with a law, regulation, subpoena, or court order.
  • Safety and security: to protect rights, property, users, and the integrity of the platform.
  • Business changes: in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets (with notice where required).

4. Uploads & media (Cloudinary)

If you upload images or documents (for example, profile images, verification documents, or check deposit images), we may store them using third-party media storage services such as Cloudinary.

  • What we store: the media file (image/document), a URL to access it, and identifiers (e.g., a “public_id”).
  • Why we store it: to provide the feature you requested (e.g., verification, recordkeeping, or support).
  • Access control: media access is restricted based on your account permissions and our internal controls.

Tip: Avoid uploading sensitive information unless it’s required for the service you’re using.

5. Cookies & sessions

We use cookies and similar technologies to keep you signed in, remember preferences, and improve security. Examples include:

  • Authentication cookies: to maintain your session (often httpOnly for security).
  • Security features: to detect suspicious activity and prevent abuse.
  • Preferences: to remember basic settings where applicable.

You can control cookies via your browser settings. Some features may not work properly if cookies are disabled.

6. Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect personal information. These may include encryption in transit (HTTPS), access controls, monitoring, and secure credential handling.

  • Least-privilege access for staff and systems.
  • Monitoring, logging, and alerting for suspicious activity.
  • Secure storage practices for sensitive credentials.

7. Data retention

We retain information for as long as needed to provide the service, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements.

  • Account data is kept while your account is active and for a reasonable period after closure.
  • Uploaded media may be retained for verification, audit, or support needs.
  • Logs may be retained for security, debugging, and compliance purposes.

8. Your rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to:

  • Request access to your personal information.
  • Request correction of inaccurate information.
  • Request deletion of certain information (subject to exceptions).
  • Object to or restrict certain processing activities.
  • Request a copy of your data in a portable format, where applicable.

To make a request, contact us at info@barclaystandardplc.pro. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling requests.

9. Children’s privacy

Our services are not directed to children under the age of 13 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction). If you believe a child has provided us personal information, contact us and we will take appropriate steps.

10. International transfers

Your information may be processed in countries other than where you live. When we transfer data internationally, we take steps designed to ensure appropriate safeguards are in place.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If changes are material, we will provide notice as required. The “Last updated” date at the top indicates when this policy was last revised.
